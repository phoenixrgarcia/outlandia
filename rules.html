Court Rules

Gameplay guidance for the Outlandia table

How to Play:

You decide whom to talk to and when. You also decide what you will tell them, and whether you want to tell them the truth or not. Though, that goes for everyone else in the game as well.

In-game you will pretend to be someone else, your character, in a fictional setting. You will meet and interact with other characters and everyone will be trying to achieve their own goals. No script is provided - it’s up to you!

Game Mechanics & Rules:
Mechanics

  • Fighting: To attack another player, you must have a weapon or appropriate ability, be within touching distance, and win a rock-paper-scissors match.
  • Success: The victim becomes unconscious.
  • Failure or tie: No effect. The defender may attack back or flee (cannot be attacked again for 10 minutes).
  • Recovery: A First Aid Kit heals instantly. Otherwise:
    • Before Last Call: recover after 10 minutes.
    • After Last Call: death occurs after 10 minutes.
  • Who goes first: The player who declares first attacks first. Simultaneous actions are decided by the event host.
  • Multiple combat: The event host determines order; resolve attacks one at a time.
  • Formal Duel: Players may duel using best-of-3 rock-paper-scissors. Required props are provided if needed. Winner renders opponent unconscious. Special abilities may allow winning on draws.
States
  • Alive: No deaths occur before Last Call unless specified by the event host.
  • Well: Not affected by poison or unconsciousness.
  • Impervious: All attacks against you fail.
  • Mithridatism: Immune to poison for 10 minutes.
  • Unconscious: Sit still. Recovery follows the same 10-minute rules based on Last Call.
  • Poison:
    • You lose all abilities.
    • Requires an Antidote to cure.
    • After Last Call: death in 10 minutes.
    • Before Last Call: death occurs 10 minutes after Last Call.
Healing
  • First Aid Kit: Instantly restores unconscious players. Cannot treat poison.
  • Antidote: Required to cure poison. Can be used by anyone but must be crafted by a qualified player.
  • Restrictions: Players cannot attack unconscious characters.
  • Searching: Players may search unconscious characters via the event host (items and money only).
  • Poison Use: Can be given directly or secretly administered via the event host.
Death
  • No deaths occur before Last Call (unless ruled otherwise).
  • Dead players receive a “Last Dying Breath” action.
  • Dead players cannot reveal secrets, clues, or abilities.
  • Only items and money may be taken from the deceased.
Arrest
  • TODO law-role placeholders may make a limited number of arrests.
  • Arrested players are placed in a holding cell.
  • Evidence must be presented or release occurs after 10 minutes (5 during Last Call).
  • Lawmen may confiscate items as evidence.
  • Arrested players cannot use abilities.
  • Escape: Possible via outside help, clever plans, or host-approved challenges.
Last Call
  • Announced during the final hour of the game.
  • Players now can die from all causes.
  • Poison and injuries take effect after 10 minutes.
  • Players must complete objectives before time runs out.

Tips For Enjoying the Game:

  • Three basic qualities: Playing a murder mystery requires only three basic attributes: a sense of fun, an ability to strike up conversation, and the willingness to step into the shoes of someone else for an evening. If you can do these things, you should have no trouble.
  • Dress the part: You will receive information, including costume tips about your character beforehand. A good costume helps you get into the spirit of the game, be sure to take lots of pictures!
  • Read your character information: Your character sheet explains who you are, who you know and what your objectives are. However, your character sheet describes what your character believes, which might not necessarily be true. Keep your character sheet handy, or at the very least in mind, as you will probably need to refer to it during the game.
  • Your goals: Your goals describe the objectives that your character is trying to achieve during the game. You should attempt every goal, even though it is unlikely you will achieve them all, as other characters may be in opposition to you.
  • Read your character information again: This time, think about your goals. You should also take note of other characters, as you will probably need to speak to them sooner rather than later.
  • Introduce yourself: Once the game begins, you will need to talk to people whilst in character. The easiest way is to introduce yourself as your character to someone else. If you do not know anything about them, you can ask who they are and why they are here. If you do know something already, whether from your character sheet or from someone else, then you can ask them about that.
  • Tips: Meant to give you an idea of things you should do at the start of the game. These tips are optional, but they may help you get started.
  • Find your friends and your enemies: In this game there are those that will help you, and those that will oppose you. It is imperative you find both. Although your friends are there to help you, you may have to help them in return.
  • Ability & Action Cards: You will have special ability cards that you can use to help achieve your goals. The cards are self-explanatory and will generally have a limited amount of uses. You should think carefully about who and what you want to use them on.
  • TODO Host Role: Generally, you should not need the event host to achieve your goals, as talking and negotiating with others can achieve most things. But you may consult the host if you have a question about the rules, or if it is required to further your goals.
  • Act the part: In-game, try your hardest to maintain the illusion that the game is genuine. Do not start talking about football, current affairs, or anything else that is not really part of the game.
  • Read your character information again: If you find yourself at a wit’s end, find a quiet place to re-read your character sheet. You may find that there is someone you have not spoken to yet, or something you need to do.
  • The murder: Everyone’s goal is to answer the question: “Who carried out the murder?” The solution is a matter of talking to people, picking up the clues, and carrying out negotiations for information.
  • In conclusion: Good luck, have fun, do not die!
